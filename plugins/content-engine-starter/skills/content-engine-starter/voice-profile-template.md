# Voice Profile

Fill this out once. It's the single most important file in the kit. Every draft is only as much "you" as this is. Be specific and honest, not aspirational.

Tip: the fastest way to fill this is to paste your 10 best posts into a model and ask it to infer each section, then correct it by hand.

---

## Who I am (one line)
> e.g. "I build AI content systems for agencies. Builder, not a consultant."

## Who I'm talking to
> The exact person. Role, company size, what keeps them up at night.
> e.g. "Agency owners, 5-30 people, who are the only one bringing in deals."

## What I sound like
- Register: (blunt / warm / dry / technical / plain-spoken?)
- Sentence rhythm: (short and punchy? long and winding? mixed?)
- Person: first-person "I built / I shipped", never "my client did"
- Formality: (swears? casual asides like "honestly" / "anyway"? or clean?)

## Words and tics I actually use
> Real phrases pulled from your writing. e.g. "point is", "nah", "tbh",
> "the math", "on rails". These are fingerprints. List 8-12.

## Things I would never say
> The off-voice tells. e.g. "unlock", "leverage", "game-changer",
> "in today's fast-paced world", "thrilled to announce".

## My opinions (the takes only I'd say)
> 3-5 positions you'll defend that others in your space won't. This is
> what makes content yours instead of anyone's.

## How I open
> Your real hook patterns. e.g. "a number + a moment", "a flat contrarian
> line", "a specific thing a client said".

## How I close
> e.g. "an instruction", "a number", "an open question I haven't solved".
> NOT a quotable aphorism.

## Proof I can stand on
> Real receipts: metrics, named tools, timeframes, client outcomes you
> can reference. The drafts pull from here so they're never hollow.
