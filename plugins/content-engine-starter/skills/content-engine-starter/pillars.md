# Content pillars

What to post about, so the feed isn't random and isn't all one note. Each idea gets tagged to a pillar before it's written. The weights keep the mix right over a month.

These weights are tuned for an agency owner whose goal is booked calls, not vanity reach. Adjust to your business.

| Pillar | Weight | What it is | Example shape |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diagnostic** | 35% | Name a problem your buyer has and how you'd find it. The core. Builds "this person gets my situation." | "Most agency owners think they have an X problem. They have a Y problem." (earned, not as the literal opener) |
| **Build-in-public** | 25% | What you're actually building/shipping, with real detail. Proof you do the work. | "I rebuilt my lead routing this week. Here's what broke." |
| **Anti-slop / POV** | 20% | The contrarian takes only you'd say. The moat against sounding generic. | "AI content fails for one reason most people won't admit." |
| **Owner POV** | 5% | The reality of running the business. Relatable, human, sparing. | "The part of agency growth nobody warns you about." |
| **Case / receipts** | 15% | A real outcome with numbers. Quiet proof, not a brag. | "$4,200/mo to $180/mo. Same automations. 90 days." |

## How to use it

- Tag every idea with a pillar at intake.
- Over any 10 posts, aim for roughly the weights above.
- If you're drifting (e.g. all build-in-public, no diagnostic), the mix is the fix, not more volume.

## What's deliberately at 0

- **Newsjack / AI-news takes.** Generic macro-AI commentary reads as filler and pulls the wrong audience. Skip it unless a launch genuinely changes your buyer's world.
- **Engagement bait.** "Agree?" posts, hot takes for the sake of it. They spike reach and book zero calls.

## The metric

Tie the whole thing to **booked calls**, not impressions. A post with 60 views that books one fit call beats a post with 6,000 views that books none. Optimize the mix toward the pillars that put qualified people on your calendar.
