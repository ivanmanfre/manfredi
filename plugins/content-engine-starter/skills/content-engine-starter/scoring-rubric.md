# Scoring rubric

Not every idea deserves a post. This decides which ones do, before you spend effort drafting. The engine scores every incoming idea and keeps the best. In practice that means archiving roughly 3 out of every 4 ideas. The bar is buyer-fit, not how clever the idea sounds.

## Score each idea 1-10 on three axes

**1. Buyer fit (x2 weight) — would my actual buyer care?**
- 9-10: speaks directly to a problem your ICP feels this week
- 5-6: adjacent, interesting to your peers but not your buyer
- 1-3: about your industry's inside baseball, or about you, not them

**2. Specificity — is there something real to say?**
- 9-10: a concrete number, a real moment, a named tool, a clear outcome
- 5-6: a decent take but mostly general
- 1-3: a vibe, a platitude, nothing to stand on

**3. Differentiation — can only you say this?**
- 9-10: a take or proof that's yours, that a competitor couldn't post
- 5-6: true but anyone could say it
- 1-3: generic advice that's everywhere already

## The formula

```
score = (buyer_fit * 2) + specificity + differentiation   // out of 40
```

- **30+** → write it
- **22-29** → hold; can it be sharpened with a real receipt?
- **under 22** → archive. Don't write it.

## Hard rejects (regardless of score)

- Coaching/course/guru framing, or anything that reads as selling a "program"
- Generic AI-news with no angle for your buyer
- A claim you can't back with something real
- A topic you've posted in the last 2 weeks (rotate)

## Why cull so hard

Volume is not the goal, booked calls are. Ten sharp posts that sound like you beat thirty that sound like anyone. The scorer's job is to protect your feed from the forgettable middle.
